---
title: "TIL: iTerm2 Remembers Your Windows So You Don't Have To"
date: 2026-02-25 02:32:38 -0500
---

Every time I restart my laptop, the first thing I do is open iTerm2 and set up my workspace. Same windows. Same tabs. Same directories. Same profiles and colors. Every single time.

It's one of those minor annoyances that never quite crosses the threshold into "I should fix this," so you just keep doing it. For years, apparently.

Turns out iTerm2 has had a feature for this the whole time. Window > Save Window Arrangement. That's it. You set up your windows and tabs the way you like them, save the arrangement, and next time you need it, Window > Restore Window Arrangement brings it all back.

And it restores *everything* — the windows, the tabs, the tab titles and colors, the current directory in each tab, even the iTerm profile used for each panel. It's not just remembering geometry, it's remembering context.

The really nice touch: you can save your preferred arrangement as the default, and iTerm2 will just open that way automatically. No restore step needed. You restart, iTerm opens, and your workspace is already there.

I've been using iTerm2 for years and never noticed this. Sometimes the best tool improvements are the ones that were already there, waiting for you to look at the menu bar.
